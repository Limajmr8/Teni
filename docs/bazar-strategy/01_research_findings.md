# BAZAR STRATEGIC ANALYSIS — PART 1: RESEARCH FINDINGS

## RESEARCH TASK 1: IMT HIGHWAY CURRENT STATUS

### Completion & Timeline
- **Overall: ~70% complete but functionally BLOCKED.** Indian side (to Moreh): ready. Thai side: complete. Myanmar side: severely delayed by civil war.
- **Realistic completion: 2028–2029 at earliest**, not 2027. The 69-bridge Tamu-Kyigone-Kalewa (TKK) section and Kalewa-Yagyi upgrade are stalled due to active conflict zones.
- **No Trilateral Motor Vehicles Agreement (IMT-TMVA) exists yet.** Even if the road were built tomorrow, there's no legal framework for cross-border cargo vehicles.

### What's Crossing at Moreh Today
- Moreh-Tamu border point is *technically* operational but constrained. Trade is mostly informal: betel nuts, textiles, second-hand goods, Chinese electronics entering India. Formal bilateral trade through this corridor has been declining since the 2021 coup.
- No significant NE India artisan/agricultural exports are currently flowing through Moreh in any organized way.

### EXIM Bank / APEDA Schemes
- **No active EXIM Bank scheme specifically tied to IMT Highway opening.** EXIM Bank's Lines of Credit to Myanmar are largely frozen post-coup.
- **APEDA Financial Assistance Scheme (FAS)** is active through 2025-26 with **higher assistance rates for NE India (up to 75%)** for export infrastructure, packhouses, processing units, and market development. This is usable NOW, independent of IMT.

### Hard Reality Check
> **The IMT Highway is a 2029+ play, not a 2027 play.** Myanmar's political situation shows no signs of stabilizing. The Tatmadaw controls the Sagaing Region that the highway passes through — active resistance territory. BAZAR should design Layer 4 as an **option**, not a dependency. Don't spend a rupee on it until the TMVA is signed.

**Recommendation:** Pivot Layer 4 planning toward **APEDA-backed organic/GI-tagged exports to Dubai, Singapore, and Bangkok via air cargo from Guwahati/Dimapur airport.** The APEDA FAS 75% subsidy for NE India makes air export viable even at small volumes. The IMT Highway becomes upside optionality, not the plan.

## RESEARCH TASK 2: PM VISHWAKARMA — MARKETING LINKAGE GRANTS

### Current Status (as of late 2025)
| Metric | Number |
|---|---|
| Applications submitted | 2.7 crore+ |
| Successfully registered (3-stage verification) | ~30 lakh |
| Completed skill training | ~23 lakh |
| Loans sanctioned | ~4.7 lakh |
| Loan amount sanctioned | ~₹4,000–4,100 crore |
| Toolkits distributed | ~6 lakh |

### The Disbursement Gap
- **Loan conversion rate: ~15.7%** (4.7L loans out of 30L registered). This is terrible. Most artisans who register are NOT getting loans.
- Primary rejection reasons: existing liabilities, poor credit history, non-compliance with verification steps.
- The ₹15,000 toolkit grant has better reach (6L+ distributed) — this is the easier lever for BAZAR.

### NE India Representation
- **Likely underrepresented.** The scheme requires Aadhaar-based registration through Common Service Centres (CSCs). NE India has lower CSC density and lower Aadhaar seeding rates. No NE-specific stats were published, which itself is a red flag.
- This is BAZAR's opportunity: **become the CSC-equivalent that helps Naga artisans register.**

### Can BAZAR Become a Technology Partner?
- **Not directly as "Technology Partner" — that role doesn't formally exist in the scheme.** But:
  - **Training Partner (TP)**: BAZAR can register through Skill India Digital Hub (SIDH) to conduct training
  - **Marketing Support**: The scheme has a marketing/brand promotion component managed by MSME Ministry. BAZAR can position as a **marketplace aggregator** that provides market linkage
  - **Real path**: Register as a Training Partner through SIDH → get authorized to enroll Naga artisans → help them get toolkits + loans → become their sales channel
- **Ministry desk**: Ministry of MSME, with scheme portal at pmvishwakarma.gov.in

### BAZAR's Play
1. Register as Training Partner on Skill India Digital Hub
2. Help artisans through 3-stage verification (using the Agriculture Dept. connection for introductions)
3. The ₹15,000 toolkit grant → fund vacuum sealers for smoked pork artisans (see Decision 2)
4. The ₹3L loan → working capital for artisans to build inventory that BAZAR buys
5. Revenue: facilitation fee per successful registration + guaranteed purchase offtake

|---|---|---|---|

| Tripura | 6th | — | Leader |
| Assam | 7th | — | Leader |
| **Meghalaya** | 10th | 27th overall | Challenger |
| **Manipur** | Lower tier | — | **Aspirer** |

### Key Insight
**Nagaland is the highest-ranked NE state for export readiness.** This is ammunition for government conversations, investor decks, and APEDA applications. The report credits:
- Policy-driven export strategy
- Organic farming support
- Niche products: minerals, handloom, bamboo

### Bottlenecks Identified for NE States
1. **Cold chain infrastructure** — near-zero organized cold chain in Nagaland
2. **Connectivity** — surface logistics slow and unreliable
3. **Documentation complexity** — artisans/FPOs lack export documentation capability
4. **Quality certification** — FSSAI, NPOP, organic certification gaps
5. **Limited cold storage** — Snowman has a hub in Guwahati only; nothing in Nagaland

### APEDA NE India Mandate
- **APEDA FAS provides up to 75% subsidy for NE-based exporters** for infrastructure, certification, and market development
- **PM-DevINE scheme**: 250 organic farming clusters being created across NE, training 25,000 farmers
- **MOVCDNER** (Mission Organic Value Chain Development for NE Region): provides financial assistance for organic inputs + FPO formation
- **NPOP certification is mandatory** for any product exported as "organic"

## RESEARCH TASK 4: CARGO INSURANCE FOR INFORMAL LOGISTICS

### What Exists
Both Digit Insurance and ICICI Lombard offer **Marine Cargo / Inland Transit Insurance** that covers domestic land transport. Despite the name "marine," this covers road transport including hill routes.

### Product Types
| Type | Use Case | Fit for BAZAR |
|---|---|---|
| **Single Transit Policy** | One-off shipment | Good for high-value heritage items |
| **Open Policy / Open Cover** | Annual policy, multiple shipments auto-covered | **Best fit** — one policy covers all Sumo parcels for a year |

### Coverage
- Vehicle accidents, overturning (critical for hill routes)
- Theft and pilferage
- Natural calamities (landslides, floods)
- Route-specific risk assessment available

### Premium Structure (estimated for ₹500–₹5,000 parcels)
- **Standard inland transit: 0.1%–0.5% of declared value**
- For a ₹5,000 parcel: ₹5–₹25 premium
- For a ₹500 parcel: ₹0.50–₹2.50 (below minimum premium threshold)
- **Practical minimum premium: ₹10–₹25 per parcel** regardless of value
- Hill route surcharge likely applies (higher risk of overturning)
- **Realistic per-parcel cost with hill surcharge: ₹15–₹50**

### Key Constraints
1. **Claims require documentation**: invoices, packing lists, photos of damage. Sumo drivers won't do this.
2. **"Insufficient packaging" exclusion**: Insurer can deny claims if packaging is poor — BAZAR must standardize packaging
3. **Informal transport = grey area**: These policies assume a "transporter" relationship. The Sumo syndicate is informal. BAZAR needs to formalize the relationship enough to qualify.

### Recommendation
- **Open Cover policy with ICICI Lombard** (they have broader rural presence than Digit)
- BAZAR holds the master policy as the shipper/merchant of record
- Per-parcel insurance is embedded in the delivery fee (customer doesn't see it separately)
- Premium cost: **₹20–₹40/parcel**, embedded in a ₹50–₹80 logistics fee
- Claims process: BAZAR handles claims centrally (driver just takes photo on WhatsApp, BAZAR files claim)
- **This is a revenue opportunity**: charge customer ₹50 for "Parcel Protection" that costs BAZAR ₹20
